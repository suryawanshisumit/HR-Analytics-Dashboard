Source: Synthetic/Internal HR Information System (HRIS) Dataset.

5. Features / Highlights
Business Problem High employee turnover is costly and disruptive. HR departments often struggle to pinpoint why top performers leave or which departments have the lowest engagement levels, leading to reactive rather than proactive talent management.

Goal of the Dashboard

To visualize the current organizational landscape and identify attrition hotspots.

To provide deep-dive filtering by department, education level, and job role.

To identify key factors (like distance from home or monthly income) that lead to employee exits.

Walkthrough of Key Visuals

Attrition Overview: High-level KPI cards showing total attrition count and percentage.

Attrition by Department: A bar chart identifying which sectors (e.g., Sales, R&D) are losing the most talent.

Employee Demographics: Breakdown of the workforce by age group, gender, and education.

Satisfaction Heatmap: A matrix showing how environment and job satisfaction impact the decision to leave.

Salary & Tenure Analysis: Visuals checking if lower-paid employees or specific tenure groups are at higher risk of leaving.

Business Impact & Insights

Retention Strategy: Identifies the "at-risk" employee profile to allow for targeted retention interviews.

Cost Reduction: By lowering attrition, the organization saves significant recruitment and training costs.

Workforce Planning: Helps leads predict future hiring needs based on historical exit trends and department growth.
